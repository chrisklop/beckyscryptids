Between November 1966 and December 1967, the people of Point Pleasant, West Virginia reported over 100 sightings of a winged, grey, red-eyed figure roughly the size of a man. It chased cars at fifty miles an hour. It hovered. It vanished.

The local paper, the *Athens Messenger*, gave it a name: Mothman. The town gathered around the legend the way towns do — half terrified, half thrilled to be on the map at all.

On 15 December 1967, the Silver Bridge across the Ohio River collapsed during rush-hour traffic. 46 people died. Mothman, the story now went, had been an omen. John Keel wrote a bestseller. A 2002 movie kept the legend alive. Today there is a 12-foot stainless-steel Mothman statue in downtown Point Pleasant, paid for by the chamber of commerce.

The most-cited mundane explanation? A migrating sandhill crane that had wandered far off course. Becky finds this unconvincing.
