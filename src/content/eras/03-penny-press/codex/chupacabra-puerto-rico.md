In March 1995, eight sheep in Moca, Puerto Rico were found dead with three small puncture wounds in the chest and most of their blood gone. Over the next few months hundreds of similar livestock deaths were reported across the island.

The witness who shaped the legend was Madelyne Tolentino, who described a creature with spiny ridges, red eyes, kangaroo legs, and grey alien skin. She named it the *chupacabra* — the goat-sucker.

Years later, Tolentino acknowledged that her description was directly inspired by the alien creature from the film *Species*, which she had seen in theaters just before her sighting. Later "chupacabra" sightings in Texas and Mexico turned out to be coyotes and dogs with severe sarcoptic mange.

But the name stuck. Three decades later it is one of the most-recognized cryptids in the Americas. Tabloid covers love a goat-sucker.
