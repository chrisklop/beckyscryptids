On 20 October 1967, Roger Patterson and Bob Gimlin filmed 53 seconds of 16mm color footage along Bluff Creek in northern California. The footage shows a tall, dark, bipedal figure striding along a gravel bar, glancing once at the camera, and walking on.

It became the single most famous piece of cryptid evidence in history. Every subsequent Bigfoot conversation refers back to it. Frame 352 — *the* glance — has been studied frame-by-frame for half a century.

A man named Bob Heironimus claimed in 2002 that he was the figure, wearing a custom suit built by costumer Philip Morris. Morris said he sold Patterson the suit. Neither claim has ever been fully corroborated, and Gimlin to his dying day swore the film was authentic.
