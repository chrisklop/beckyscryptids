The Swedish ecclesiastic Olaus Magnus spent twelve years engraving a map of the Nordic seas — the *Carta Marina*. He drew every detail he could verify, and where he could not verify, he drew monsters.

Whales the size of islands. A sea pig with hooves. A wide-eyed *Rosmarus* hauling itself up a cliff. A spotted whale called *Balena* swallowing a ship. The sea-bishop, the prister, the *Ziphius*. Each one labeled with a scholarly Latin caption.

The map was reproduced as a definitive reference for two hundred years. The monsters traveled into atlases, into bestiaries, into Pontoppidan's Natural History, and eventually into every cabinet of curiosities in Europe. A single confident scholar can make-believe an entire ocean.
