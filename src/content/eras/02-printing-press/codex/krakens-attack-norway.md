Erik Pontoppidan, Bishop of Bergen, devoted a chapter of his 1752 *Natural History of Norway* to the kraken. He cited every sailor who would talk to him. The creature, he wrote, was *"perhaps the largest creature in the world"* — backs as large as small islands, tentacles tall as a ship's mast.

Pontoppidan was no fool. He understood that some of his sources were drunks. But the testimony was consistent across centuries and across coasts, and he published. For the next hundred years every European child knew the kraken from the bishop's book.

Modern marine biologists eventually identified the real animal hiding behind the tales: *Architeuthis dux*, the giant squid. Pontoppidan was, in his way, right.
