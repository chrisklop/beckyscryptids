In 1717, Louis Renard published *Poissons, Écrevisses et Crabes… aux Isles Moluques et sur les Côtes des Terres Australes* — a folio of 460 hand-colored fish from the East Indies. It is mostly accurate and beautiful. Sandwiched among the parrotfish and groupers is plate 57: a mermaid.

The caption is straightforward — *"a monster resembling a Siren"*, caught off the coast of Amboina, lived four days in a vat of water, and *"refused all the food which was offered to it."* Renard names the colonial official who captured it. He gives the latitude.

For seventy years the plate was reprinted, copied, and cited as authoritative natural history. A mermaid, certified by the Dutch East India Company.
