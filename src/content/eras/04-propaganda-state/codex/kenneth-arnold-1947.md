On 24 June 1947, the businessman and amateur pilot Kenneth Arnold was flying his private CallAir A-2 over the Cascade Mountains in Washington State when he saw nine bright objects flying in formation near Mount Rainier. He estimated their speed at over 1,200 mph — faster than any known aircraft.

Arnold described their motion to a reporter as *"like a saucer if you skip it across water."* The reporter coined the phrase **flying saucers** that same afternoon. It went out on the AP wire and into history.

Within weeks Americans were seeing them everywhere. Within two weeks, the Roswell crash report broke. The modern UFO era did not exist on June 23. By July 8 it was the dominant tabloid story in the country, and it has never quite left.
