On 28 August 1995, Fox aired *Alien Autopsy: (Fact or Fiction?)* in prime time, hosted by Jonathan Frakes. The centerpiece was seventeen minutes of black-and-white footage purporting to show two military pathologists dissecting a humanoid body recovered from Roswell. Eleven million Americans watched. Fox ran it twice more that year.

The footage came from a London-based music-video producer named Ray Santilli. He swore for over a decade that it was authentic 1947 film he had bought from a retired military cameraman.

In 2006 Santilli admitted on camera that the footage was a *reconstruction* he had filmed in a flat in Camden. The latex body had been built by sculptor John Humphreys in three weeks; the organs were sheep brains and chicken-shop offal. Santilli's defense: there *had* been real footage, but it degraded, so he reshot it.

The footage continues to circulate. The reconstruction is now itself a piece of historical evidence — of how cheaply, and how convincingly, you could fake the modern UFO age with a domestic camcorder.
