On 14 November 2004, off the coast of southern California, F/A-18F Super Hornets from the USS *Nimitz* carrier group recorded a small, smooth, white object — described by pilots as a *"tic tac"* — accelerating, hovering, and outmaneuvering them. The cockpit gun-camera footage was unremarkable in its own right; what was remarkable was that it survived.

For thirteen years the recordings sat inside the Defense Department. In December 2017 the *New York Times* revealed the existence of the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP), a quietly-funded Pentagon office for studying unexplained military aerial encounters. The Pentagon released three short videos. The phrase "UAP" — Unidentified Aerial Phenomena — replaced "UFO" in government parlance.

In 2023 the former intelligence officer David Grusch testified to Congress under oath that the US government had recovered "non-human biologics." The Pentagon denied it. The hearings continued.

For the first time since 1947, the official position was no longer that nothing was up there. The official position was that *something* was, and they did not yet know what.
