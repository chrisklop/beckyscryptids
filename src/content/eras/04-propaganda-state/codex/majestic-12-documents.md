In December 1984, the UFO researcher Jaime Shandera received an unsolicited envelope at his Los Angeles home. Inside was a roll of 35mm film. The film contained eight pages of what appeared to be a 1952 briefing to President-elect Eisenhower describing a covert committee of twelve American officials — "Operation Majestic 12" — formed to manage the alien recoveries at Roswell.

The documents listed the twelve members by name: Vannevar Bush, Roscoe Hillenkoetter, James Forrestal, and so on. Every name was real. Every signature looked right. The story they told was that the United States government had been quietly handling alien remains since 1947.

The FBI investigated and concluded the documents were *"completely bogus."* Skeptics pointed to anachronistic terminology, mismatched typefaces, and signatures lifted from earlier letters. Believers pointed out that anyone who could *prove* the documents were forged would have to know what the real documents looked like.

The argument is unresolved forty years later, which is exactly how this kind of document is supposed to work.
