In the snow-locked forests of the northern Algonquian peoples, a wendigo was not just a monster — it was a warning. A traveler who tasted human flesh in a starving winter would become one: gaunt, frozen, never satisfied. The cure for hunger was the cause of it.

The wendigo is the patron saint of every "warning carved into a cave wall" — a story so useful, so terrifying, that whole nations agreed never to test it.
