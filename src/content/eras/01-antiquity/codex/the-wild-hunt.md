From Scandinavia to the British Isles to the Alps, the same story echoed: in deep winter, a spectral host rode the sky — hounds baying, horns wailing, a king or god at the head of the column. Odin led it in the north. King Arthur led it in the west. The Devil led it where the church had taken hold.

To see the Wild Hunt was bad luck. To be swept up in it was worse. Villagers shuttered their doors and listened, and the standing stones outside town stood witness to every passing.
