Medieval bestiaries described a creature stitched together from spare parts: the head and neck of a serpent, the body of a leopard, the haunches of a lion, the feet of a hart. From its belly came a noise like *thirty couple of hounds questing*.

Arthurian knights chased it for their entire lives and never caught it — which was, of course, the point. Some legends are designed to never be solved.
