Across Europe, the fae were never called fae to their faces — they were *the Good Folk*, the *Gentry*, the *Fair Ones*. To speak the true word was to invite them in. When a sickly child was born, or a healthy one began to fade, the explanation came easy: the fae had swapped the real child for a changeling.

The story was kinder than the truth, and the truth was usually grief. The fae took the blame, and the household kept its iron, salt, and a sprig of rowan by the door.
