A 17th-century Yorkshire ballad describes a dragon plaguing Wantley — eating children, livestock, and trees. The hero, More of More Hall, commissions armor studded with spikes and confronts the beast. The dragon laughs. They wrestle for two days. More finally kicks it in *the only soft spot* — its rear end — and the dragon dies.

Modern readers note the ballad was almost certainly satire about a real lawsuit between landowners. Even in the 1600s, the people knew: every monster story is also a story about whoever told it.
